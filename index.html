Frequently asked questions

What is the Goethe-Zertifikat C2?

The Goethe-Zertifikat C2: Großes Deutsches Sprachdiplom (GDS) is the highest German language certification available. It certifies near-native proficiency — the ability to understand virtually everything, express yourself spontaneously with precision, and handle literary and academic texts. It's required for teaching German at universities, professional translation work, and positions demanding absolute language mastery.

How long is the Goethe C2 exam?

The written portion takes approximately 4 hours: reading (80 min), listening (35 min), and writing (80 min). The speaking module adds about 15 minutes, conducted in pairs. Total exam time exceeds 4 hours.

What score do I need to pass?

You need at least 60 out of 100 points. Each module must be passed independently. The C2 has a notably high failure rate — thorough preparation is essential.

How does the AI writing review work?

Submit your literary analysis, summary, or argumentative essay and receive detailed feedback calibrated to C2 standards. The AI assesses content depth, stylistic sophistication, grammatical precision, and whether your text demonstrates the near-native command expected at this level.
